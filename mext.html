1. Do I have to reach out to the consulate before or after getting accepted?
You should contact the consulate after receiving your acceptance letter to begin the visa process. It's essential to have all required documents ready by then.

2. What should I do after submitting the application on the website?
After submitting, keep track of your email for any updates or interview calls. Meanwhile, gather required documents and be ready for next steps.

3. How many months does it take to get accepted?
The process usually takes around 3 to 6 months, depending on the program and country.

4. Do I have to go to a test center to write the English proficiency test?
Some tests, like TOEFL iBT, can be taken online, but others like IELTS require you to visit a test center.

5. What is the acceptance rate for MEXT master's students from India?
The acceptance rate is competitive, typically ranging between 15% to 25%, depending on the program and year.


6. Can I apply for multiple scholarships at once?
Yes, applying for multiple scholarships is allowed, but make sure to read the terms carefully to avoid conflicts.

7. Is work experience required for master's programs?
While not mandatory for all programs, work experience can boost your application, particularly for business or engineering fields.

8. How can I prepare for the MEXT interview?
Research the program and be clear about your goals. Showing a solid understanding of Japanese culture and study interests will strengthen your chances.

9. Can I apply for MEXT if I don't know Japanese?
Yes, many MEXT programs are offered in English. However, learning basic Japanese is encouraged and beneficial for daily life.

10. Is there an age limit for MEXT scholarships?
Yes, for most programs, applicants should be under 35 years old. For undergraduate programs, the limit is usually 24 years old.

11. Does MEXT ask for a research plan after the application is accepted?

No, the research plan is required at the time of application, not after acceptance.


When is the Research Plan Needed?

  • The Field of Study and Research Plan is one of the most important documents you submit during the initial application process for the MEXT Research Scholarship.
  • It helps the selection committee evaluate your academic goals, clarity of thought, and the feasibility of your proposed research.

Why is it Important?

  • The research plan demonstrates your preparedness and understanding of the field you wish to pursue in Japan.
  • A well-written plan increases your chances of being shortlisted for interviews or the next stage of the selection process.

After Acceptance:

  • Once accepted, you may need to refine or expand the plan under the guidance of your advisor in Japan.
  • However, the core idea should remain consistent with what you submitted originally.

ðŸ“„ Documents Required for MEXT (Research Students)

The following documents are required when applying for the MEXT scholarship as a research student:

  1. Application Form:
    • Complete the official MEXT application form, which is available on the Japanese embassy's website.
  2. Field of Study and Research Plan:
    • A detailed proposal explaining your intended research, goals, methodology, and relevance to your field of study.
  3. Academic Transcripts:
    • Transcripts from your undergraduate and (if applicable) graduate studies.
    • Ensure these are certified and translated into English or Japanese if required.
  4. Recommendation Letters:
    • At least one or two letters from professors or academic supervisors who can attest to your academic performance and research potential.
  5. Health Certificate:
    • A medical examination form completed by a certified physician, verifying you are in good health.
  6. Degree Certificate or Graduation Letter:
    • A certificate of graduation or a letter indicating your prospective graduation date.
  7. Proof of Language Proficiency:
    • Proof of English proficiency (TOEFL, IELTS) or Japanese proficiency (JLPT) is often recommended.
  8. Abstract of Thesis (if applicable):
    • If you have written a thesis as part of your previous studies, an abstract or summary of your thesis is required.
  9. Passport Copy:
    • A copy of your passport (the identification page) to confirm your nationality.
  10. Photograph (3.5cm x 4.5cm):
    • A recent passport-sized photo, typically attached to the application form.

Note: Document requirements may vary slightly depending on the country or the Japanese embassy you are applying through. Always check the official embassy or consulate website for the most up-to-date requirements.

What Do I Have to Attest?

When applying for the MEXT scholarship, you will need to attest (certify) certain documents to ensure their authenticity. Here are some common documents that you may need to attest:

Documents to Attest

  • Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts from your previous educational institutions. These should be attested by the issuing institution or a notary public.
  • Degree Certificates: Copies of your degree certificates (e.g., bachelor's degree). These should be attested by the issuing institution or a notary public.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Letters from your professors or mentors. These should be signed and sealed by the recommender.
  • Proof of English Proficiency: If required, provide proof of your English proficiency (e.g., IELTS or TOEFL scores). These should be attested by the issuing organization.
  • Passport Copy: A copy of your passport, attested by a notary public.
  • Medical Certificate: A medical certificate attested by a licensed medical practitioner.
  • Research Proposal or Study Plan: If applicable, provide a research proposal or study plan. This document does not usually require attestation but should be well-prepared and signed by you.
  • Photographs: Passport-sized photographs as specified in the application guidelines.

Note: Be sure to verify the specific requirements for each document as they may vary by institution or country.
